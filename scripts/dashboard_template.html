About this work

Project: Before the Breakpoint — detecting hidden AMR escalation from longitudinal MIC distribution drift.

Data: Pfizer ATLAS surveillance database, non-USA partition, 2004–2024. 845,288 isolates from 82 countries, with MIC values for 30 antimicrobials and β-lactamase genotyping on a subset.

Method, in brief. Most AMR surveillance uses categorical S/I/R interpretation, which by design fires only after a clinical breakpoint has been crossed. We instead track movement of isolates above the epidemiological cutoff (ECOFF, the upper edge of the wild-type distribution) but not yet above the clinical breakpoint. We aggregate these subthreshold signals into the Pre-Resistance Alert Score (PRAS), an L2-regularised logistic regression with features for current level, recent trend, and acceleration of the ECOFF-exceeding fraction. The model is trained on 2007–2014 predictor years and validated on 2015–2019 — proper out-of-time prospective forecasting.

Scope conditions. The framework works for pathogen-drug pairs where resistance is built incrementally (carbapenem resistance from porin loss + efflux + low-activity carbapenemases; novel β-lactam/BLI escape via point mutations). It does not work for single-step horizontal-gene-transfer resistance such as most CTX-M ESBLs against ceftriaxone, where MICs jump from <0.06 to >64 mg/L with nothing intermediate to "creep" through. The lead-time analysis (Phase 2 results) cleanly identifies which 7 of the 17 modelled pairs are framework-applicable.

Validation strength. Out-of-time test AUC = 0.902, AUPRC = 0.848, against a base rate of 33.8%. Cells with PRAS > 0.75 had a 97% subsequent crossing rate; PRAS < 0.10, 8%.

Limitations. ATLAS is not population-based — participating sites change over time. The 2016 broth-microdilution standardisation distorted longitudinal colistin testing (excluded). Countries that joined surveillance late (e.g. Argentina) are systematically under-flagged for events that preceded their participation. Confidence intervals on the PRAS itself are not provided; a Bayesian successor with posterior intervals is in development.
